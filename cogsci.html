What is Cognitive Science?

Date Published: February 8, 2024

Whenever someone learns that my major is cognitive science, they either express approval or look confused. For the latter, I'd provide a memorized quick, digestible explanation: psychology and computer science combined. However, is that accurate?

This morning, I was talking with a friend who is taking the same introductory cognitive science course as me. Before class started, our professor was talking about how cognitive science and psychology are distinct. That reminded my friend of how whenever someone asks her what cognitive science is, she doesn't know how to answer, which she told me with a laugh. That made me think: what is cognitive science?

Cognitive science is an interdisciplinary field that draws from six disciplines: psychology, neuroscience, computer science, linguistics, philosophy, and anthropology (the study of human development across eras and cultures).

It is an exciting field nowadays because of all of the achievements regarding artificial intelligence (AI), mental health treatments, and more. In addition, tons of research disciplines benefit from taking the interdisciplinary approach that cognitive science offers. For example, psychology research and neuroscience research go hand in hand, informing each other of the brain's influences on our emotional and cognitive processes and vice versa.
